    7. Red Flags to Avoid Offshore Betting Sites
      1. Lack of Proper Licensing: Always verify that the site is licensed and regulated by a recognized U.S. state gaming authority (e.g., Michigan Gaming Control Board, the New Jersey DGE or the Pennsylvania Gaming Control Board). Unlicensed and offshore operators are not legal and offer no player protections.
      2. No Secure Payment Options: Reputable online betting sites support secure and trusted payment methods (Visa, Mastercard, Play+, PayPal, ACH, etc.). Sites without these familiar payment options or requesting cryptocurrency-only deposits or payouts should be avoided.
      3. Unrealistic Bonuses and Promotions: Offers that sound too good to be true or with incredibly high wagering requirements (like 10x or 20x) should be avoided.
      4. Poor User Reviews or No Public Reputation: A lack of online presence or consistent negative feedback about payment delays, poor customer service or account closures is a serious red flag.
      5. Missing Responsible Gambling Tools: Legitimate sites promote responsible gambling with deposit limits, self-exclusion options and visible links to problem gambling resources. If you don't see these tools, it may indicate a lack of consumer protection focus.

    10. Responsible Gaming on Sports Betting Sites

      The top US sportsbooks place a strong emphasis on responsible gambling, offering a variety of tools and resources to support users who may struggle with problem gambling.

      Responsible Gambling Tools at Sportsbooks

      Most online sportsbooks allow users to:

      1. Set Deposit Limits: Control the amount you can deposit in a specific time frame.
      2. Wagering Limits: Restrict the amount you can bet in a single day, week, or month.
      3. Time Limits: Monitor and limit the amount of time you spend on the platform.
      4. Self-Exclusion: Temporarily or permanently block yourself from using the platform if necessary.

      These measures are in place to ensure that gambling remains an enjoyable form of entertainment and does not become a financial or emotional burden.

      Additional Responsible Gambling Support

      Responsible gambling means recognizing when betting is no longer fun and taking steps to regain control. The National Council on Problem Gambling (NCPG) offers confidential, 24/7 support through multiple channels:

      1. Phone: Call the helpline at 1-800-522-4700
      2. Text Message: Send a message to the same number for support
      3. Live Chat: Access live chat support on the website
